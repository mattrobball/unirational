# Genus-8 projection ansatz: exact secant obstruction

## Outcome

A projected genus-8 Mukai sixfold can carry the desired seven-dimensional
family of lines in \(\mathbf P^9\), but the explicit projection below cannot
lie on any smooth hypersurface, of degree 9 or of any other degree.  Two
smooth source points have the same image, and the two projected tangent spaces
span all of \(\mathbf P^9\).  Consequently every equation vanishing on the
image has zero differential at that image point.

This is a rigorous null result for this structured ansatz, not a proof of the
Debarre--de Jong conjecture.

## Construction

Use Pluecker coordinates in the order

\[
(p_{12},p_{13},p_{14},p_{15},p_{16},p_{23},p_{24},p_{25},p_{26},
 p_{34},p_{35},p_{36},p_{45},p_{46},p_{56}).
\]

Let \(Y=\operatorname{Gr}(2,6)\cap L\), where \(L\simeq\mathbf P^{12}\)
is cut out by

\[
\begin{aligned}
\ell_1={}&p_{13}+2p_{14}+p_{16}-p_{23}+2p_{25}-2p_{26}
 +p_{35}+p_{36}-p_{45}+2p_{46}+p_{56},\\
\ell_2={}&p_{14}+p_{15}-p_{16}+2p_{23}+p_{24}+p_{26}
 -2p_{35}+p_{36}+2p_{45}+p_{46}-p_{56}.
\end{aligned}
\]

Take

\[
a=p_{12},\qquad b=p_{34}
\]

and project from the plane \(\mathbf P(K)\), where

\[
K=\operatorname{span}\{c,u,v\},\quad
c=p_{12}+p_{34},\quad
u=-3p_{13}+p_{46}+p_{56},\quad
v=2p_{14}-p_{25}-2p_{36}.
\]

All three center vectors satisfy \(\ell_1=\ell_2=0\), so
\(\mathbf P(K)\subset L\).  The projection is

\[
\pi:Y\longrightarrow \mathbf P(L/K)=\mathbf P^9.
\]

## Exact certificates

### Smooth source

The source is smooth in characteristic zero.  The script checks the reduction
modulo 101 on all fifteen standard Pluecker charts.  On each chart it writes
\(Y\) as two equations in the eight affine coordinates of
\(\operatorname{Gr}(2,6)\) and verifies that the ideal generated by those two
equations and the \(2\times2\) minors of their Jacobian is the unit ideal.
Thus the characteristic-101 fiber is smooth.  Proper specialization of the
projective Jacobian singular scheme then certifies that the characteristic-zero
fiber is smooth as well.

### Center misses the source

On \(K\), with coordinates \((s,t,r)\) relative to \((c,u,v)\), three of the
Pluecker quadrics are

\[
s^2,\qquad -3t^2,\qquad -3t^2+4r^2.
\]

They have no common projective zero in characteristic zero.  The script also
checks this by saturating all fifteen restricted Pluecker quadrics by
\((s,t,r)\).  Hence \(\mathbf P(K)\cap Y=\varnothing\), so \(\pi\) is a
morphism.

### Double point with spanning tangents

Because \(c=a+b\in K\),

\[
y:=\pi(a)=\pi(b),
\]

with \(a\ne b\).  The affine-cone tangent spaces to \(Y\) at \(a\) and
\(b\) each have vector-space dimension 7.  Both lie in \(p_{56}=0\); their
intersection has dimension 2, and therefore their sum has dimension 12:

\[
T_aY+T_bY=L\cap\{p_{56}=0\}.
\]

Since \(u\) has nonzero \(p_{56}\)-coordinate,

\[
T_aY+T_bY+K=L.
\]

Equivalently, the two tangent spaces to the two image branches at \(y\) span
the full ten-dimensional vector space \(L/K\), hence all of \(\mathbf P^9\).

If a homogeneous polynomial \(F\) vanishes on \(\pi(Y)\), its differential at
\(y\) annihilates both projected tangent spaces.  Those spaces span the target,
so \(dF_y=0\).  Thus every hypersurface containing \(\pi(Y)\) is singular at
\(y\).  In particular, searching degree-9 members is unnecessary for this
ansatz: none can be smooth.

The same argument is a useful general test.  If
\(X\subset\mathbf P(E)\) is projected from an external
\(\mathbf P(K)\), two distinct points \(x_1,x_2\in X\) have a common image,
and their affine tangent cones \(U_i=\widehat T_{x_i}X\) satisfy

\[
U_1+U_2+K=E,
\]

then every hypersurface containing the image is singular at that common
image.  When the secant point is Terracini-general, this is precisely the
condition that the center meet the secant variety transversely.

For a general genus-8 section

\[
X=\operatorname{Gr}(2,6)\cap\mathbf P^{12},
\]

the secant variety is the restriction of the rank-at-most-four Pfaffian
cubic, hence is an eleven-dimensional cubic hypersurface in \(\mathbf P^{12}\).
Indeed, at two general disjoint two-planes the affine Grassmannian tangent
spaces each have dimension nine and meet in dimension four; after a general
codimension-two linear section, the source tangent spaces each have dimension
seven, meet in dimension two, and span a twelve-dimensional vector space.
Terracini gives secant dimension eleven, and irreducibility of the general
restricted Pfaffian cubic gives equality.  A general external plane center
meets its Terracini-good locus transversely.  Thus the general genus-8
projection to \(\mathbf P^9\) is obstructed as well.  An arbitrary special
center still needs an explicit transverse secant; the calculation above
provides one for the displayed center.

## The seven-dimensional line family survives projection

Lines on \(\operatorname{Gr}(2,6)\) are parameterized by
\(\operatorname{Flag}(1,3;6)\), of dimension 11.  Requiring a line to lie in
each of the two hyperplanes defining \(L\) gives four equations, so every
nonempty component has dimension at least 7.  This locus is nonempty here: the
line

\[
\langle p_{12},-p_{13}+p_{15}+p_{16}\rangle
\]

lies in \(Y\).

Because the center misses \(Y\), the projection is defined by a basepoint-free
subspace of the ample bundle \(\mathcal O_Y(1)\), whose pullback from the target
is again \(\mathcal O_Y(1)\).  It therefore has no positive-dimensional fiber
and is finite.  Each source line maps isomorphically to a target line, and only
finitely many source-line components can lie over any one target line.
Consequently the image still has a line-family component of dimension at
least 7.

## Reproduction

From the repository root, run

```sh
M2 --script experiments/genus8_secant_obstruction.m2
```

With Macaulay2 1.26.06 the run takes under one second and prints

```text

explicit source line certified: true
source line-family dimension lower bound: 7
projection center disjoint from Gr(2,6): true
vector tangent dimensions at a,b: 7,7
projection immersive at both branches: true
vector dimension of their span: 12
dimension after adjoining the center: 13
projected tangent spaces span P^9: true
```
